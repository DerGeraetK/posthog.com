## What we do

The demand gen team owns paid ads at PostHog. Because we're already such a brand and content driven company, our job is to go reach the people those channels can't, and pull them into the PostHog universe.

This quarter all of that points one direction. We're not marketing our products one at a time anymore. Every one of them is a great tool on its own, but it's even better when it ladders up into self-driving, so every channel we run is really a way to get more people to hear the good word.

### Why this team exists

We're in a strong spot today. We've got a healthy flow of high quality inbound leads driven by word of mouth and general brand love, but that won't last forever, and we can't rest on it.

Organic already brings in loads of leads, but ads are how we reach up market and educate our ideal customers on the value of PostHog and self-driving. Our job now is new signups, and especially the signups that rank high for our ICP and are likely to turn into MRR.

Sure, we've got a ton of fans who are going to love all of this, and they're going to tell their friends, but what about everyone else? Everyone four degrees from Kevin Bacon instead of three? We want to get them psyched too, and we want them as customers.

We're SUPER lucky to have so much great word of mouth driving our growth, but we can't just sit back and coast. We have to get self-driving out there through targeted ads, in front of the people who'll use it to its fullest.

### How we think about paid

We split our budget roughly 50/50 between awareness and conversion, and we run conversion ads on search (Google, Bing, ChatGPT) alongside awareness plays on LinkedIn, Reddit, YouTube, and Connected TV. This quarter we're widening that mix as we push self-driving at the top of the funnel.

Unlike most demand gen teams we don't run ads to book demos, because we make it easy for people to self serve without ever talking to sales, so funneling them toward a demo would just be a waste. We also don't touch third party trackers or pixels, it's against our brand and values, so all of our conversion tracking runs through our own privacy first setup instead. The full detail on channels, tracking, creative, landing pages, and the monthly growth review lives in the [Paid ads overview](/handbook/marketing/paid).
