The **SupportHog** Microsoft Teams bot connects your Teams tenant to PostHog Support, enabling two-way sync between Teams conversations and support tickets.

- **Teams to PostHog** – messages in configured Teams channels automatically create support tickets and add comments
- **PostHog to Teams** – team replies in PostHog are posted back to the original Teams thread

This is separate from PostHog's main integrations. SupportHog has its own bot, credentials, and event handling.

## Connecting Microsoft Teams

1. Go to [**Support > Settings**](https://app.posthog.com/support/settings) in PostHog
2. Under **Microsoft Teams**, click **Connect Microsoft Teams**
3. A Microsoft authorization window opens – sign in and grant the SupportHog app access to your tenant

<CalloutBox icon="IconInfo" title="Admin required" type="fyi">

Connecting Microsoft Teams requires a Teams admin to authorize the SupportHog app. Only organization admins in PostHog can manage the Teams integration.

</CalloutBox>

## Adding support channels

After connecting, you can configure up to 25 Teams channels to monitor for support tickets. You can add one channel per Teams group.

1. In [**Support > Settings**](https://app.posthog.com/support/settings), under **Support channels**, click **Add Teams channel**
2. Select a **team group** from the dropdown – this lists all Teams groups the authorized user has joined
3. PostHog installs the SupportHog app into the selected group (this is automatic and idempotent)
4. Select a **channel** within that group
5. The channel appears in your configured list

To remove a channel, click the trash icon next to it. Existing tickets from that channel aren't affected, but new messages will no longer create tickets.

Click **Refresh** to reload the list of available team groups or channels if you've recently made changes in Microsoft Teams.

## How tickets are created

There are two ways a Teams message becomes a support ticket:

### Support channel messages

Any top-level message in a configured support channel automatically creates a new ticket. Thread replies on that message sync as ticket messages.

### Bot mentions

Type `@SupportHog` in any channel to create a ticket from that message. This works in any channel, not just configured support channels. The bot mention feature is always active when Teams is connected.

Both methods:

- Set `channel_source` to `teams` on the ticket
- Resolve the Teams user's name and avatar
- Convert Teams message formatting to PostHog's rich text format
- Post a confirmation reply in the Teams thread with the ticket number

<h2 id="org-catalog">Org catalog setup</h2>

If your Microsoft tenant's app setup policy blocks the public Teams Store, PostHog can't automatically install the SupportHog bot. When this happens, you'll see a warning banner:

> **SupportHog isn't available in your Microsoft tenant's app catalog.**

To resolve this, a Teams admin needs to upload the SupportHog app package to your organization's app catalog:

1. Get the SupportHog app package (`.zip` file) from the PostHog team
2. Open the [Microsoft Teams admin center](https://admin.teams.microsoft.com/)
3. Go to **Teams apps > Manage apps**
4. Click **Upload new app** and select the SupportHog `.zip` file
5. Return to PostHog and click **Retry install**

This is a one-time setup per Microsoft tenant.

## Viewing Teams tickets

Teams-sourced tickets appear in your [inbox](https://app.posthog.com/support/tickets) alongside widget, email, and Slack tickets. Each Teams ticket shows:

- A Teams icon indicating the channel source
- The Teams user's name and avatar on messages

You can filter the ticket list by channel source to see only Teams tickets.

## Disconnecting

To remove the Microsoft Teams integration:

1. Go to [**Support > Settings**](https://app.posthog.com/support/settings)
2. Click **Disconnect Microsoft Teams** at the bottom of the Microsoft Teams section
3. Confirm the disconnection

Existing tickets created from Teams aren't affected – they remain in your inbox but no longer sync new messages.
