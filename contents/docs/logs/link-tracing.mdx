Logs and [Tracing](/docs/tracing) both record what happens inside your application, but from different angles. Logs capture what happened at each point; traces show how a request flowed across services and where time went. When they share a `trace_id`, you can jump between them to get both views of the same operation.

## Navigate from a log to its trace

When a log entry has a `trace_id` attribute, a **View trace** button appears in two places:

1. **On the log row** – A button in the floating action bar when you hover over a log entry
2. **In the log details modal** – A button in the modal header when you click to expand a log entry

Clicking either button opens the originating trace in the Tracing scene. If the log also has a `span_id`, the trace view auto-highlights the span that emitted the log.

This button only appears when:
- The log entry has a `trace_id` attribute
- Your team has [Tracing](/docs/tracing) enabled

## Navigate from a span to related logs

When viewing a trace, you can jump to logs emitted during that operation:

1. Open a trace in the Tracing scene
2. Click on a span to expand its details
3. Click the **View logs** button

This opens the Logs viewer filtered to logs with the same `trace_id`, scoped to a 10-minute window (±5 minutes) around the span's timestamp. The time window keeps the query efficient while capturing logs from the traced operation.

This button only appears when:
- The span has a `trace_id`
- Your team has [Logs](/docs/logs) enabled

## Ensure logs and traces are linked

For logs and traces to link, they need to share the same `trace_id`. If you're using OpenTelemetry for both logging and tracing, this happens automatically – the OTel SDK propagates trace context to log records emitted within an active span.

If your logs aren't linking to traces:

1. **Check your OpenTelemetry setup** – Make sure your logging SDK is configured to include trace context. Most OTel logging libraries do this by default when there's an active span.
2. **Verify the `trace_id` attribute** – In the log details view, check that the log has a `trace_id` attribute matching your traces.
3. **Check feature flags** – Both Logs and Tracing need to be enabled for your team.
